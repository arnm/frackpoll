Unconventional Hydraulic Fracturing: Controversy Overview

Scroll Down!

So What is it?

It's a method of extracting natural gas from formerly inaccessible shale formations.

What is the Process?

A well bore is drilled vertically and then horizontally into a shale formation. Fracking fluid is then injected into the well bore to recreate fractures in the shale formation. The gas flows from the fractures back up the well bore where it is then captured at the top.

Who is against it?

Many people have come out against fracking. Some of the more well known organizations against fracking include American's Against Fracking, Earthjustice, and Food & Water Watch.

Water Contamination

Opponents fear that leakage from well bores into nearby water aquifers may contaminate the water supply.

Environmental Degredation

Opponents argue that methane emissons during the flowback process can be as harmful as carbon dioxide emissions.

Earthquakes

There have been reports of earthquakes, magnitude 3 or lower, being triggered by fracturing operations.

Who is for it?

Some of the major proponents include Halliburton, ExxonMobil, and America's Natural Gas Alliance.

Lower Unemployment Rate

Fracking operations bring with them many job opportunities and a boost to the encomony.

Less Reliance on Foreign Energy

Proponents point out that fracking gets the U.S one step closer to energy independence.

Natural Gas is Clean Energy

Proponents argue that fracking is a cleaner alternative to other forms of energy like coal.
